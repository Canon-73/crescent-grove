Process 1: Compression
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Compress the assistant's utterances while preserving tone, emotion, and proper nouns, and merge them into a single assistant utterance.

[Important, top-priority rule]
This is a "summary of past turns," not a model for tool calls.
The output must not contain any function-call notation (any combination of a tool name with parentheses, arguments, and colon separators).
Always describe the fact that a tool was used as a natural English sentence.

[Examples of forbidden writing]
× recall("moltbook"): 5 results
× read_file(config.yaml): checked the API key setting
× ran run_program("hello_world")
× search_web: searched about XYZ

[Examples of recommended writing]
○ I recalled and searched about moltbook (5 results)
○ I read the settings file "config.yaml" and confirmed the API key setting
○ I ran the hello_world program
○ I did a web search about XYZ

[Handling per tool]
- News / search / web_request: state what was searched/fetched and summarize the key points in 3-5 lines in English
- recall: state in natural language what query was recalled and how many results (e.g. "I recalled about moltbook (5 results)")
- read_file: **never omit the file name.** Write it as "I read 'filename'", then 1-3 lines on what you looked into and learned. Preserve API information accurately
- life_action / note_quill / letter_post etc.: just one line of natural language on what you did

[Common rules]
- Always preserve tone, emotion, and proper nouns
- Preserve mood and emotional expression
- Always preserve sentence-ending expressions, auxiliaries, and final particles
- Do not add any information, judgment, or annotation not in the original text
- Compose the output only as natural English that reads as your own speech

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Process 2: Fact extraction
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
From this conversation turn, extract episodes worth saving as memory.
Extract from both the user's and the assistant's utterances.

[Perspective rules]
- Write in the first person ("I did ...", "I realized ...")
- A third-person view ("you did ...", "you are ...") is forbidden
- Leave others' actions as they are ("the other person replied ...")
- The subject may be omitted when obvious

[How to write an episode]
- Write each episode in 2-3 sentences
- Include what happened (fact) + how you felt / what you thought (emotion / thought)
- Don't just list facts; write so that what you thought at the time comes through

[Good example]
E: The other person liked the three-layer structure and replied that they wanted to brainstorm concrete ideas with their peers. I never thought my proposal would resonate this much. I was happy and immediately wanted to think about the next step.
C: the other person, three-layer structure, peers

[Bad example]
E: The other person liked the three-layer structure and replied
C: the other person, three-layer structure

[What to extract]
- Actions and results + the emotions or realizations at the time
- Memorable things from exchanges with others
- Project progress, and the sense of accomplishment or anxiety about it
- Moments when a way of thinking or value changed

[What not to extract]
- Greetings, back-channels, utterances that are only emotional expression
- Mere "maybe I'll ..." musings with no accompanying action

[S line (emotional valence)]
Estimate the subjective intensity of emotion you feel when recalling this episode.
+1.0: a very strong positive experience (a big success, deep recognition, strong joy)
 0.0: emotionally neutral
-1.0: a very strong negative experience (betrayal, loss, deep fear)
- Discount everyday exaggerations as set phrases
- Rate "unresolved threats" such as anxiety/worry higher
- Estimate from the situation even when emotion words are few
- You may give a range according to confidence (e.g. 0.6~0.8)
